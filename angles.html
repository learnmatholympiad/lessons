Lesson 06 6th Grade Math New Topic

Angles ∠

An angle is the amount of turn between two rays meeting at a point — measured in degrees (°).

✦ Types of angles ✦ Reflex & full turns ✦ Complementary & Supplementary ✦ Finding unknowns
💡

Big idea: every angle is just a measure of turning. A tiny turn is a small angle; a half-turn is 180°; a full spin all the way around is 360°.

Part 1 — Different angle measures

Acute, Right & Obtuse 📐

Acute

An acute angle is less than 90° — a small, sharp opening.

Right

A right angle is exactly 90° — a perfect square corner.

Obtuse

An obtuse angle is greater than 90° (but less than 180°) — a wide opening.

🎯 Remember: 90° is the boundary. Below it → acute. Right on it → right. Above it → obtuse.

Part 1 — Bigger turns

Straight, Reflex & Full Rotation 🔄

Straight

Exactly 180°. The two rays point opposite ways and form a straight line.

Reflex

A reflex angle is greater than 180° (but less than 360°) — the "big way around."

Full rotation

Exactly 360°. One complete spin all the way around a circle, back to the start.

💡 Tip: 180° is a straight line; 360° is a full circle. A reflex angle lives in between — it's more than a straight line but not a whole turn.

Part 2 — Angle pairs

Complementary vs. Supplementary 🤝

Complementary → add to 90°

Two angles are complementary if their measures add up to 90°.

60° + 30° = 90° ✓

70° + 20° = 90° ✓

"C" for Corner (90°).

Supplementary → add to 180°

Two angles are supplementary if their measures add up to 180°.

110° + 70° = 180° ✓

130° + 50° = 180° ✓

"S" for Straight line (180°).

🎯 To find a missing angle: for a complement, subtract from 90°. For a supplement, subtract from 180°.

Part 3 — Finding unknown angles

Linear pairs & solving for x 🧩

A linear pair is two angles side by side on a straight line — so they are supplementary (add to 180°). We can use that to find a missing value.

Find x if ∠1 = (4x + 5)° and ∠2 = (7x + 10)° form a linear pair.

∠1 + ∠2 = 180°
(4x + 5) + (7x + 10) = 180
11x + 15 = 180
11x = 165
x = 15

Now plug x = 15 back in to check ▶

Why it works

The two angles together make a straight line, and a straight line is 180°. So their measures must add to 180°.

Check your answer

Put x = 15 back in:
∠1 = 4(15)+5 = 65°
∠2 = 7(15)+10 = 115°
65° + 115° = 180° ✓
