Fractions ½

A fraction is a part of a whole — a way to describe how much of something you have.

Big idea: the top number (numerator) counts how many parts you have; the bottom number (denominator) says how many equal parts make one whole.

Part 1 — The basics

What is a fraction? 🍕

Read it out loud

3/4 = "three fourths" or "three quarters."

The bigger the denominator, the smaller each piece — 1/8 is smaller than 1/4.

📏

Fractions are everywhere

Half a pizza, a quarter of an hour, three-fifths of a tank — all fractions describing part of a whole.

🎯 Remember: a fraction is just a division waiting to happen — 3/4 also means 3 ÷ 4.

Make them by multiplying

Like vs. Unlike

Like fractions share a denominator: 1/7, 2/7, 3/7.
Unlike fractions have different ones: 2/3, 6/7, 3/5.

Part 2 — Operations

Adding & Subtracting ➕➖

Same denominator? Easy!

Add (or subtract) the numerators, keep the denominator.

1/5 + 3/5 = (1+3)/5 = 4/5

3/5 − 1/5 = (3−1)/5 = 2/5

Different denominators?

First rewrite them with a common denominator, then add.

1/2 + 3/7

= 7/14 + 6/14

= 13/14

💡 Tip: multiply each fraction by the other's denominator to get a common one — here 1/2 and 3/7 both become out of 14.

Part 2 — Operations

Multiplying & Dividing ✖️➗

Multiply — straight across

Top × top, bottom × bottom. Then simplify.

3/4 × 2/5

= (3×2)/(4×5) = 6/20

= 3/10 (simplified)

Part 3 — A special kind

The Unit Fraction superpower ⚡

Multiplying by 1/n divides by n

A unit fraction has numerator 1. Multiplying a number by 1/n just divides it by n.

1/5 × 10 = 10/5 = 2

1/3 × 9 = 9/3 = 3

The Unit Fraction Rule

Any unit fraction splits into two smaller equal unit fractions:

1/n = 1/(2n) + 1/(2n)

1/3 = 1/6 + 1/6 = 2/6 = 1/3 ✓

💡 Why it matters: unit fractions are the building blocks of many Math Olympiad problems — spotting them makes hard problems simple.

Part 3 — Fractions of numbers

Fractional parts — the twin of percentages 👯

"of" means multiply

A fraction of a number works just like a percent of a number.

1/2 of 36 = 18

= 50% of 36 ✓

Multiply the whole by the numerator, divide by the denominator.

Worked examples

1/3 of 18 = 18 ÷ 3 = 6

4/5 of 20 = 20 ÷ 5 × 4 = 16

5/6 of 30 = 30 ÷ 6 × 5 = 25

The steps 🪜

  1. Divide the whole by the denominator.
  2. Multiply by the numerator.
  3. That's your fractional part!
